How It Works

Five real-world ways agents and brokerages use the Neighborhood Explorer to win more buyers.

Use Case 1

Before A Showing

Send a private link to the widget before a viewing so buyers arrive already educated on the area. They spend less time asking “what’s around here?” and more time deciding if the home is right.

Use Case 2

After A Showing

Follow up with a branded PDF or private link. Buyers can revisit the neighborhood details later with their partner or family without going down a Google rabbit hole.

Use Case 3

Neighborhood Discovery

When a buyer is undecided on neighborhoods, generate widgets for a few options and send them as private links. They can compare commute times, points of interest, and key stats on their own.

Use Case 4

At The Property, On Your Phone

Pull up the widget on your phone or tablet at a showing. Walk buyers through schools, lifestyle spots, and commute times live while you’re standing in front of the house.

Use Case 5

Lead Capture On Your Website

Add the widget with a lead form to your listings and neighborhood pages. Buyers unlock the insights by entering their details, and those leads go straight into your CRM.

Frequently Asked Questions

We are here to help you. If you have any questions about Dream Neighborhood please reach out to support@dreamneighborhood.com.

What exactly is Dream Neighborhood?
Dream Neighborhood is a neighborhood data widget that embeds directly on your real estate website. When a buyer views a listing, they can instantly access local demographics, schools, commute times, walkability scores, points of interest and more — without ever leaving your site.
How difficult is it to install?
Not difficult at all. It’s a single code snippet — roughly 3 lines — that you or your web developer paste into your website once. That’s it, as simple as a Meta Pixel. We also provide setup documentation and are happy to help if you get stuck.
Will it work with my current website?
Yes. The widget works with virtually any website platform including WordPress, Squarespace, Wix, and custom built sites. If you’re unsure, just ask us during a demo.
Where does the data come from?
We aggregate from best-in-class sources including the U.S. Census Bureau for demographics, Redfin for housing market data, Walk Score for mobility scores, NCES for school data, and Google Maps for points of interest.
How is this different from Zillow or Redfin?
Zillow and Redfin have neighborhood data — but it lives on their websites, alongside competing listings and other agents. Dream Neighborhood puts that same context directly on your website, keeping buyers engaged with you rather than sending them to your competitors.
What does it cost?
We offer simple monthly plans starting at $39/month for solo agents and $149/month for brokerages. No contracts, cancel anytime.
What if my website is managed by a developer or IDX provider?
No problem. We provide a simple code snippet your developer can add in minutes. We’re also happy to jump on a quick call with them directly to make installation as smooth as possible.
