Frequently Asked Questions

Everything agents and brokerages ask about Dream Neighborhood. Still stuck? Email support@dreamneighborhood.com.

What exactly is Dream Neighborhood?
Dream Neighborhood is a neighborhood data tool that embeds directly on your real estate website. When a buyer views a listing, they can instantly explore local demographics, schools, safety, commute times, walkability, points of interest and more, without ever leaving your site.
How difficult is it to install?
Not difficult at all. It’s one line of code that you or your web developer paste into your website once. That’s it, about as simple as adding a tracking pixel. We also install it for you for free and are happy to help if you get stuck. See our step-by-step installation guide for the exact code and setup.
Will it work with my current website?
Yes. It works with virtually any platform: WordPress, Squarespace, Wix, Webflow, custom-built sites, and the major IDX providers. If you’re unsure, just ask us during a demo.
Popup or embedded: which should I use?
The popup is our flagship: one line of code adds a neighborhood button to every listing, perfect for keeping your current design. The embedded explorer drops a full-width, interactive panel directly into a page when you want neighborhood data front and center. You can use either, or both.
Can I capture leads with it?
Yes. Turn on the optional built-in lead form and buyers enter their name, email, and phone to unlock the full neighborhood report, turning high-intent browsers into contacts that land straight in your inbox and dashboard.
Can I roll it out across my whole brokerage?
Yes. Invite your whole team to a shared account, with no per-seat licenses. Pricing scales on views and reports, not users, so you can grow your team without renegotiating seats, and see analytics by agent from one dashboard.
What does it cost?
Plans start at $24.92/month for solo agents and $99.92/month for brokerages on annual billing (or $39 and $149 month-to-month). Keep just one buyer a year on your site instead of losing them to Zillow and the subscription pays for itself.
